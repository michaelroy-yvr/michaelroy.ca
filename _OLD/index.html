Michael Roy is a Vancouver-based advertising and campaign strategist with fifteen years of experience in political and advocacy campaigns.

Since January 2024, Michael has served as the Director of Direct Communications and Engagement with the B.C. Government's Government Communications and Public Engagement agency.

In 2019, Michael teamed up with his long-time colleague Robin Steudel to co-found Metric Strategies - a boutique advertising and marketing agency specializing in public engagement and persuasion projects for the non-profit and political sectors. As Managing Director of the agency, Michael helped lead multiple award-winning projects to fight for public school teachers, help non-profits raise money online, and organize non-union workers.

Michael was previously Director of Research and Strategy at Point Blank Creative where he helped build a regional video production company into an national advertising agency.

He also served as Digital Director for Canada's NDP during the 2015 election. His team set new national fundraising records and mobilized tens of thousands of volunteers and supporters across the country.

Prior to his time in Ottawa Michael served as Director of Communications for the BC NDP, where he directed award-winning ad campaigns, developed communications strategies for breakthrough by-elections and modernized the party's communications tactics. Michael has worked on election campaigns and referenda at the municipal, provincial and federal level.

When he's not at work, Michael can be found in the kitchen or playing wit his daughter.
